Toor Dall Consumption Rate in India

India is the largest consumer and producer of pulses in the world. Toor Dall (pigeon pea) is one of the most consumed pulses in India with an annual consumption of around 4.0 to 4.5 million tonnes. It is a staple in Indian diets, especially in Maharashtra, Karnataka, Madhya Pradesh, and Andhra Pradesh.

Growth and Expansion Plans

The Indian pulses industry is expected to grow at a CAGR of 6-7% in the next 5 years. Key drivers include rising urbanisation, increasing health awareness, and government support for agri-processing industries. There is a shift toward modern dal milling technologies, hygienic packaging, and traceable sourcing.

Scope for Entrepreneurs & Millers

  • Setting up Toor Dall mills in production belts (Vidarbha, MP, Karnataka)
  • Developing brand identity and entering modern trade chains
  • Creating backward linkages with farmers for consistent supply
  • Introducing value-added products (protein mixes, ready-to-cook dals)
  • Gaining FSSAI and organic certifications to reach premium markets
